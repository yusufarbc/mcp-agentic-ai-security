## MCP and Related Works – Literature Summary

**1) Model Context Protocol (MCP): Landscape, Security Threats, and Future Research Directions** (2025, arXiv:2503.23278) – Hou, X. et al.
* **Abstract:** The study outlines the four-stage lifecycle of MCP and a threat model consisting of 16 scenarios. Risks arising from malicious developers/users, external attackers, and code errors are analyzed; actionable defense strategies and future research areas are presented for each phase.

**2) Advancing Multi-Agent Systems Through Model Context Protocol: Architecture, Implementation, and Applications** (2025, arXiv:2504.21030) – Krishnan, N.
* **Abstract:** The efficiency of standardized context and tool sharing via MCP in multi-agent scenarios is examined. Performance gains and new research opportunities are reported through case studies in different domains.

**3) A survey of agent interoperability protocols: MCP, ACP, A2A, ANP** (2025, arXiv:2505.02279) – Ehtesham, A. et al.
* **Abstract:** Four different protocols (MCP, ACP, A2A, ANP) are compared in terms of interoperability and security features. The study proposes a phased adoption roadmap involving tool access, structured messaging, delegation, and decentralized marketplaces.

**4) Model Context Protocol (MCP) at First Glance: Studying the Security and Maintainability of MCP Servers** (2025, arXiv:2506.13538) – Hasan, M. M. et al.
* **Abstract:** Through a large-scale scan, new attack surfaces (unique vulnerabilities, tool poisoning) and maintainability issues hosted by MCP servers are analyzed. The study emphasizes the need to develop MCP-specific detection tools.

**5) MCPmed: A Call for MCP-Enabled Bioinformatics Web Services for LLM-Driven Discovery** (2025, arXiv:2507.08055) – Flotho, M. et al.
* **Abstract:** It is proposed to enrich bioinformatics APIs with an MCP layer. It is argued that this approach will increase data discovery capabilities of LLM-based agents and the reproducibility of studies, and lightweight migration templates are offered for integration.

**6) Making REST APIs Agent-Ready: From OpenAPI to MCP Servers for Tool-Augmented LLMs** (2025, arXiv:2507.16044) – Mastouri, M. et al.
* **Abstract:** An automated generation method from OpenAPI specifications is proposed to reduce MCP server setup costs. It is stated that failures largely stem from contract quality, and the feasibility of automation for the MCP ecosystem is proven.

**7) MCPToolBench++: A Large Scale AI Agent Model Context Protocol MCP Tool Use Benchmark** (2025, arXiv:2508.07575) – Fan, S. et al.
* **Abstract:** A large-scale test set containing real MCP tools is presented. Success rates of agents in multi-step tool calls and context window problems are experimentally demonstrated.

**8) MCP-Guard: A Defense Framework for Model Context Protocol Integrity in Large Language Model Applications** (2025, arXiv:2508.10991) – Xing, W. et al.
* **Abstract:** A comprehensive attack benchmark is presented with a three-stage architecture that protects MCP tool interactions against prompt injection and poisoning. The proposed system is reported to provide detection with high accuracy.

**9) Help or Hurdle? Rethinking Model Context Protocol-Augmented Large Language Models** (2025, arXiv:2508.12566) – Song, W. et al.
* **Abstract:** Through a comprehensive benchmark test, it is shown that MCP integration does not always provide performance increases; it can lead to problems such as lack of proactive use and cost overruns.

**10) MCP-Universe: Benchmarking Large Language Models with Real-World Model Context Protocol Servers** (2025, arXiv:2508.14704) – Luo, Z. et al.
* **Abstract:** In tests with real MCP servers, it was found that LLM agents struggled due to long context and undefined tools, and success rates remained below expectations.

**11) LiveMCP-101: Stress Testing and Diagnosing MCP-enabled Agents on Challenging Queries** (2025, arXiv:2508.15760) – Yin, M. et al.
* **Abstract:** MCP agents were subjected to stress tests with realistic task scenarios; failure modes such as planning/coordination errors and token inefficiency were revealed and classified.

**12) Model Context Protocols in Adaptive Transport Systems: A Survey** (2025, arXiv:2508.19239) – Chhetri, G. et al.
* **Abstract:** It is discussed how fragmented structures in adaptive transport systems can be combined with context awareness and standard integration provided by MCP, and a research roadmap in this field is presented.

**13) AgentX: Towards Orchestrating Robust Agentic Workflow Patterns with FaaS-hosted MCP Services** (2025, arXiv:2509.07595) – Tokal, S. S. K. A. et al.
* **Abstract:** It is aimed to improve multi-stage agent workflows by hosting MCP tools in a Function as a Service (FaaS) architecture. Opportunities and challenges are evaluated through success, latency, and cost analyses.

**14) Automatic Red Teaming LLM-based Agents with Model Context Protocol Tools** (2025, arXiv:2509.21011) – He, P. et al.
* **Abstract:** An automated red teaming framework has been developed against MCP tool poisoning. Experiments showed that commonly used agents can be manipulated and existing defense mechanisms remain insufficient.

**15) Asset Discovery in Critical Infrastructures: An LLM-Based Approach** (2025, Electronics 14(16):3267) – Coppolino, L. et al.
* **Abstract:** Addresses asset inventory management, which is vital for cybersecurity in Critical Infrastructures (CI). The study proposes an LLM-based architecture based on the "Mixture of Experts" (MoE) model to extract asset information from unstructured data (logs, configuration files, etc.) in heterogeneous environments where traditional deterministic methods fall short. It is experimentally proven that the proposed approach reduces manual effort and strengthens the cybersecurity posture by increasing asset visibility.

**16) Integrating Generative AI and Model Context Protocol (MCP) with Applied Machine Learning for Advanced Agentic AI Systems** (2025, Int. J. of Computer Trends and Technology) – Bhandarwar, N.
* **Abstract:** Presents a hybrid architecture integrating Generative AI (GenAI) and Applied Machine Learning models via the Model Context Protocol (MCP). The paper technically examines how MCP enables the development of autonomous "Agentic AI" systems that can make context-aware decisions and adapt to dynamic environments by providing a standard communication layer between different AI models.

**17) A Survey of the Model Context Protocol (MCP): Standardizing Context to Enhance Large Language Models (LLMs)** (2025, Preprints 202504.0245) – Singh, A. et al.
* **Abstract:** MCP is evaluated as a standard context layer; interoperability gains and long-term performance uncertainties are emphasized. Opportunities and risks for different sectors are analyzed.

**18) AI Agents for Economic Research** (2025, NBER Working Paper 34202) – Korinek, A.
* **Abstract:** Examines the evolution of AI systems from simple chatbots to autonomous agents capable of planning and executing multi-step tasks from the perspective of economic research. The study demonstrates how economists can create their own research agents capable of literature review, data analysis, and econometric coding without needing programming expertise; discusses how these agents transform efficiency as "proactive research partners" and the necessity of human oversight.
